CS/01

BigOps: an operations command center for three countries

Problem

Operational truth lived in five places: a legacy order system, Airtable, spreadsheets, inboxes and people's heads. Hundreds of stuck orders, 15 live campaigns, 100+ freelancers, and no single view of any of it.

What I built

One platform where the team sees everything: campaigns, stuck orders, stock, staffing and spend across all three branches. AI reads each stuck order and suggests the reason, and it pulls from eleven systems automatically so nobody copies numbers between spreadsheets. I designed and built all of it myself.

Impact

Around 23 hours of manual reporting automated every week; twenty staff across three countries now work from one screen.

CS/02
ForecastingInventoryCost savings

Frame stock prediction: forecasting that pays for itself

Problem

Frames were bought on gut feel. Stockouts stalled framing mid-project; overstock tied up cash across three warehouses.

What I built

Predicts three months of demand from last year's pattern, the current trend and a safety margin, minus what is already on the shelf and on order. Out comes a simple answer: exactly what to buy, per frame type, per branch. An independent second check recalculates everything so the numbers can be trusted.

Impact

$30k to $40k saved per year, and reordering moved from gut feel to a defensible number.

$30k to $40k saved / yr 10 to 25% less dead stock 3 months forecast ahead

CS/03
Production planning$200k projectDelivered on time

Magnum Photos: production control for a $200k deadline

Problem

Roughly 5,000 bespoke-framed prints across nine production streams in three countries, staggered hard deadlines, and zero slack in the schedule.

What I built

A production control system that told every team exactly how much to produce each day to hit the deadline, adjusting automatically as the numbers moved. Held-up artwork was set aside without stalling the rest. It rebuilt itself overnight from the latest order data, 156,000 live calculations doing the arithmetic.

Impact

Delivered in two months, on timeline. Slippage was visible the day it happened, not the week after.

~5,000 framed prints 9 production streams 2 months, on time

CS/04
UI / UX designPrototype to production

On-hold report: redesigning triage as a visual hierarchy

Problem

Hundreds of stuck orders in one flat list: no way to see who needed action today and who had been waiting silently for a week.

What I built

I redesigned it from scratch: orders group under each client, colour-coded labels say exactly where things stand, and anything waiting too long gets flagged at the top. Built as a working prototype first, then shipped almost exactly as designed.

Impact

Triage follows the hierarchy now, and the pattern became the interaction template for the rest of the platform.

The automation toolkit: small tools, permanent wins

Problem

Dozens of small recurring chores: chasing approvals, writing up meeting actions, bulk-editing orders. Individually minor. Together, a full-time job.

What I built

Small tools that each remove one chore for good. Approvals land in Slack the second they are requested. AI reads every meeting transcript and posts the action items to the team. Another tool updates hundreds of orders on its own, in minutes instead of days.

Impact

Core contributors to the 80+ hours of manual work eliminated every week.
